Interview 6: Supply Chain Manager

Conducted By: Morgan Hardin on October 16, 2023


Who are you?

I am a 56-year-old supply chain manager who works for Caterpillar. I manage engines for our customers and I use Excel and Microsoft Office 365 every day. I try to stay up-to-date with the latest technology but I’m usually extremely busy with work. I spend my free time with my family and friends and love watching baseball.


Have you ever worked in a restaurant?

Yes, I worked in a restaurant for my first job years ago. It was a fast food restaurant, so I never worked in a restaurant where I made tips.


As a customer, what is the most frustrating thing about being a customer in a restaurant while waiting to be seated?

The most frustrating thing about being a customer is how packed the waiting area is. I like being able to sit down to wait, but sometimes it is so packed there is nowhere to sit and other times they don’t even have seating.


Does it matter to you if you are seated by a window or in a booth or table?

No, it does not matter to me where I am seated.


Would you prefer to call a restaurant to make a reservation or go through an app or website?

If I had to make a reservation, I’d rather call but I also don’t mind going through an app or website.


Is there anything frustrating or confusing about making a reservation through a restaurant? If so, what would make it easier to use?

It’s really frustrating when they make you put in your credit card to reserve a spot. I don’t want my credit card taken and if I have to put my card down, most of the time I just go somewhere else.


From what you remember from working in a fast food restaurant, what is the most frustrating part about service in the front?

I would say how rude people can be. Typically restaurants are understaffed, so one person is doing a job that normally is for about 3 or 4 people. People who have never worked in a restaurant don’t understand how difficult and stressful it can be, so when they don’t have patience and are rude about it makes the job more stressful and frustrating.


What is the most frustrating part about how service relates to the back?

If the back is running behind, it makes service in the front more difficult since there tend to be more lines and more angry customers. If the front is running behind, it makes the back more stressed about getting the order out quickly.


What can be changed to make it less frustrating?

Hiring more people and having effective training could help keep customers and employees happy.
