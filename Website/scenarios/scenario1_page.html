Scenario 1:

Jameson, Reservation Call

A customer called on Wednesday to make a reservation for four people on Friday in the same week. There was only one reservation left on Friday at 7pm, and two reservations left at 8pm. After Jameson relayed this back to the customer, they chose to reserve themselves for 8pm. Jameson used SmartReserve's worker application to reserve them for 8pm and took down their phone number for updates and confirmation.​


The SmartReserve application saved this information and texted a confirmation message to the customer. Jameson had to do little to make this work as all he had to do was read the calendar with the open spots for the designated day.​


On Friday, it was already a little past 8pm and if it gets to thirty minutes past the restaurant will not accept them and get ready for the next time slot. If this happens, SmartReserve can be marked as a "no-show". Twenty minutes late, the party arrived with four adults, the person who called approached the front desk and asked if they were ready to be seated on time at 8pm. Jameson only needed to confirm the party name and phone number with the saved information in the SmartReserve system by clicking on the day and then the time and seeing the listed reservations. The name and phone number was listed on the web application, and he was able to seat the party for the night.​
